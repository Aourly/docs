After registering with BankID, you need to complete your Worknode profile before you can start invoicing and receiving salary. This guide walks you through the required information and settings.

## Why this matters

Your profile contains the banking and tax information Worknode needs to pay your salary. Without a complete profile, Worknode cannot process payouts -- even if your invoices have been paid by clients.

## Accessing your profile

Log in to Aourly and navigate to your Worknode profile settings. This is where you enter personal, financial, and tax information.

## Step-by-step setup

### 1. Enter your address

Fill in your home address details:

- **Street address**
- **Zip code** (postnummer)
- **City**

This is used for employment records and official correspondence from Worknode.

### 2. Add your bank details

Your bank information is where Worknode deposits your net salary. You need to provide:

- **Bank name** -- the name of your bank (for example, Nordea, SEB, Handelsbanken, Swedbank)
- **Clearing number** -- the four-digit number identifying your bank branch
- **Account number** -- your personal bank account number

All bank details are stored with encryption. Only authorised Worknode staff can access them for processing salary payments.

> **Important:** Your bank clearing number (4 digits) and account number are needed for salary transfers. This is NOT the same as the invoice bank details (which are Worknode's). Invoices display Worknode's bankgiro/account since clients pay Worknode directly.

Double-check these details carefully. Incorrect bank information will delay your salary payments.

### 3. Set your income tax percentage

Your income tax percentage determines how much preliminary tax (preliminärskatt) Worknode withholds from each salary payment. The default is **30%**, which is a common approximate rate in Sweden.

Your actual tax rate depends on:

- **Your municipality** (kommunalskatt varies by kommun)
- **Your total annual income** (higher income may trigger statlig inkomstskatt)
- **Any deductions** you may be entitled to

To find your exact rate:

1. Visit [Skatteverket's website](https://skatteverket.se)
2. Use their tax calculator (skatteberäkning)
3. Or check your latest tax decision (skattsedel)

> **Warning:** Ensure your income tax percentage matches your Skatteverket tax table. Too low and you will owe tax at year end; too high and you will get a refund but have less cash monthly.

### 4. Configure pension contributions

You can optionally set a **pension percentage** for voluntary occupational pension (tjänstepension). The default is **0%**.

If you set a pension percentage, that portion of your gross salary is set aside for pension savings before your net salary is calculated. Even a small percentage adds up over time, especially if you do not have other pension arrangements through another employer.

This is entirely voluntary. You can change it at any time (subject to a change request approval).

### 5. Review and accept terms of service

Before your profile is considered complete, you must review and accept Worknode's terms and conditions. These cover:

- The employment relationship between you and Worknode
- Fee structure and salary calculation
- Responsibilities on both sides
- Privacy and data handling

Take the time to read through them. You can view the current terms version in your profile at any time.

### 6. Save your profile

Click **Save** to store your settings. Your profile is now ready for Worknode to process salary payouts when your invoices are paid.

## Important fields explained

### Income tax percentage (preliminärskatt)

This is the preliminary tax deducted from each salary payment. The default 30% is a common rate, but your actual rate depends on your municipality and income level. Getting this right is important:

- **Too low**: You will owe money when you file your annual tax declaration (deklaration)
- **Too high**: Your monthly payouts will be smaller, but you will get a refund from Skatteverket
- **Just right**: No surprises at tax time

### Pension percentage (tjänstepension)

This is voluntary occupational pension. The percentage is applied to your gross salary, and the amount is set aside for pension savings. If freelancing through Worknode is your primary income, consider contributing at least a few percent to build pension over time.

### Bank details vs. invoice payment details

It is important to understand the difference:

| Your bank details (profile)       | Invoice payment details              |
|-----------------------------------|--------------------------------------|
| Your personal bank account        | Worknode's bankgiro/account          |
| Where your salary is deposited    | Where clients pay invoices           |
| You provide these in your profile | Automatically added to invoices      |

## Updating your settings later

After the initial setup, you can update your profile at any time. However, certain sensitive changes require a **settings change request** that Worknode staff must approve:

| Setting                  | Requires change request? |
|--------------------------|--------------------------|
| Address                  | No                       |
| Bank account number      | Yes                      |
| Bank clearing number     | Yes                      |
| Bank name                | Yes                      |
| Income tax percentage    | Yes                      |
| Pension percentage       | Yes                      |

> **Info:** You can request changes to your tax and pension settings at any time through a settings change request. This process protects you against unauthorised changes to sensitive financial information.

The change request process is a security measure. You will be notified when your change request is reviewed and either approved or denied.

## Tips for getting started

- **Complete your profile immediately after registration.** An incomplete profile delays your ability to invoice and receive payments.
- **Verify your tax rate with Skatteverket.** The default 30% is an estimate. Getting the right number avoids surprises at tax time.
- **Consider pension contributions.** If freelancing through Worknode is your primary income source, setting aside even 3-5% for pension is a good habit.
- **Keep your bank details up to date.** If you switch banks, submit a change request promptly to avoid payment delays.

## Frequently asked questions

**Where do I find my tax percentage?**
Visit skatteverket.se and use their tax calculator. The rate depends on your municipality and expected annual income.

**Can I change my bank details later?**
Yes, but changes to bank details go through a change request that Worknode staff must approve. This protects against unauthorised changes.

**What happens if I set the wrong tax percentage?**
Too little tax withheld means you may owe money at year end. Too much means a smaller monthly payout but a refund later. Set the correct rate to avoid both scenarios.

**Is pension contribution mandatory?**
No. The default is 0%. It is voluntary and you can adjust it at any time through a change request.

**Are my bank details safe?**
Yes. Account numbers and clearing numbers are encrypted in the database. Only authorised Worknode personnel can access them.
