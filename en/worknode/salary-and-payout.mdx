When you invoice through Worknode, you do not receive the full invoice amount. Instead, Worknode processes the payment and pays you a net salary after deducting fees, taxes, and mandatory contributions. This guide explains exactly how that calculation works.

## The payment flow

Here is what happens after a client pays your invoice:

1. **Client pays the invoice** -- money goes to Worknode's client funds account (klientmedelskonto)
2. **VAT is separated** -- the VAT portion is set aside (it belongs to Skatteverket, not you or Worknode)
3. **Worknode deducts their fee** -- a monthly package fee and/or percentage-based fee depending on your plan
4. **Aourly's share is deducted** -- platform fee for the invoicing tool
5. **Employer contributions are calculated** -- the mandatory arbetsgivaravgifter (~31.42%)
6. **Income tax is withheld** -- based on the tax percentage in your profile
7. **Pension is deducted** -- if you have configured a pension percentage
8. **Net salary is paid** -- the remaining amount is deposited to your bank account

## Understanding the salary breakdown

Here is how each deduction works, using a concrete example. Suppose you send an invoice for 12,500 SEK including 25% VAT:

### Invoice total including VAT

This is what the client pays. In our example: **12,500 SEK**

### VAT

VAT is not your income -- it is collected on behalf of Skatteverket. At 25%, the VAT on 12,500 SEK is 2,500 SEK.

**Invoice total excluding VAT: 10,000 SEK**

### Worknode fees

Worknode charges based on your plan (Flex or Pro). This may include a monthly package fee and/or a percentage of the invoice amount excluding VAT. For this example, assume a 6% fee:

**Worknode fee: 600 SEK**

### Aourly's share

Aourly charges a platform fee. For this example, assume a small percentage:

**Aourly fee: ~200 SEK**

Remaining: ~9,200 SEK

### Employer contributions (arbetsgivaravgifter)

All Swedish employers must pay arbetsgivaravgifter, approximately 31.42%. This funds social security, healthcare, pensions, and other public benefits. The contribution is calculated from the remaining amount:

**Employer contributions: ~2,196 SEK**

Gross salary: ~7,004 SEK

### Income tax (preliminärskatt)

Income tax is withheld based on the percentage set in your profile. If your tax rate is 30%:

**Income tax: ~2,101 SEK**

### Pension contribution (tjänstepension)

If you have configured a pension percentage (for example 5%):

**Pension: ~350 SEK**

### Net salary

After all deductions:

**Net salary: ~4,553 SEK**

This is what you receive in your bank account.

> **Warning:** Your net salary will be significantly less than the invoice total. This is normal and accounts for all employer obligations. When setting your hourly rate, factor in these deductions so your take-home pay meets your needs.

## Why the deductions exist

It is important to understand that these deductions are not all "costs" in the traditional sense:

- **Employer contributions (arbetsgivaravgifter)** fund your social security benefits: sjukpenning (sick pay), föräldrapenning (parental leave), and base pension. These are benefits you receive as a Swedish employee.
- **Income tax** is the same tax everyone pays. Worknode withholds it so you do not need to pay it in a lump sum later.
- **Pension** is your own savings, set aside for retirement.
- **Worknode and Aourly fees** are the actual cost of the service.

> **Info:** Even though employer contributions reduce your take-home pay compared to invoicing with your own company, you get social security benefits in return (sjukpenning, föräldrapenning, pension). If you ran your own company, you would pay these contributions yourself as the employer.

## Payout schedule

Salary payouts are typically processed on a regular schedule -- weekly or monthly depending on your Worknode plan. Worknode collects all client payments received during the period, runs the salary calculations, and deposits your net salary to the bank account in your profile.

The exact payout date depends on Worknode's processing schedule. You can track upcoming and past payouts in Aourly.

## Revenue tracking

Aourly provides a revenue events log where you can see:

- Each invoice payment received
- The salary calculation breakdown for each payment
- Running totals of your earnings
- History of all payouts

Check this regularly to verify that deductions match your expected rates and to understand your earnings pattern over time.

## Tax reporting

Worknode handles your tax reporting as your employer:

- **Monthly**: They report your income and tax withholdings to Skatteverket
- **Annually**: Your employment income from Worknode appears on your annual tax return (deklaration)
- **Tax slips**: You receive the same kind of employer tax documentation as any Swedish employee

You do not need to file a separate business tax return. Your income from Worknode is reported as employment income (inkomst av tjänst).

## Adjusting your settings

If you want to change your tax rate or pension percentage, you can do so in your Worknode profile. Changes to financial settings require a change request that Worknode approves (see the [Profile Setup](profile-setup) guide).

Common reasons to adjust:

- **Moving to a new municipality**: Your kommunalskatt changes, so your tax rate should be updated
- **Income changes**: If your total annual income changes significantly, your tax rate may need adjustment
- **Starting or changing pension contributions**: You can start contributing at any time or adjust the percentage

## Tips

- **Verify your tax rate annually.** Municipal tax rates can change and your income level may push you into a different bracket.
- **Track your revenue events.** Regular monitoring helps you catch any discrepancies early.
- **Consider pension contributions.** If Worknode is your primary income, setting aside even a few percent builds pension savings over time.
- **Plan for the fee.** Remember that Worknode's service fee, Aourly's fee, employer contributions, and taxes together make up a significant portion of the invoice amount. When setting your hourly rate, factor in these deductions.
- **Use the salary calculator.** If Aourly provides a salary calculator, use it to estimate your net pay before creating an invoice. This helps you quote appropriate rates to clients.

## Frequently asked questions

**When do I get paid?**
After the client pays and Worknode processes the salary. Payouts follow a regular schedule (weekly or monthly depending on your plan).

**Can I see a breakdown of deductions?**
Yes. Worknode provides salary specifications and Aourly shows revenue events with a detailed breakdown for every payout.

**What if a client doesn't pay?**
Worknode handles debt collection. You may need to wait for payment before your salary can be processed, as salary is paid from received client payments.

**How is my net salary calculated?**
Start with the invoice total excluding VAT. Subtract the Worknode and Aourly fees, then calculate employer contributions. From the gross salary, subtract income tax and pension contributions. The remainder is your net salary.

**Does Worknode handle my tax return?**
Yes. They report your income to Skatteverket as your employer. Your Worknode earnings appear as employment income on your annual tax return.

**Why is my net salary so much less than the invoice amount?**
The difference accounts for VAT (which was never your income), Worknode and Aourly fees, employer contributions, income tax, and pension. This is comparable to what any employed person in Sweden experiences -- the gross-to-net difference is significant in the Swedish tax system.

**What are employer contributions (arbetsgivaravgifter)?**
A mandatory Swedish payroll tax of approximately 31.42% paid by all employers. It funds social security, healthcare, pensions, and other public benefits. As your employer, Worknode pays this on your behalf from the invoice proceeds.
