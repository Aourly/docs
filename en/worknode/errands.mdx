The errand system is your built-in communication channel with Worknode. Whenever you have a question, need help with an issue, or want to discuss something related to your employment, errands are the way to do it.

## What errands are for

Errands replace email and phone calls for Worknode-related communication. Everything is tracked in one place, with a written record you can refer back to at any time.

Common reasons to create an errand:

- **Salary questions**: "Why was my March payout lower than expected?"
- **Tax settings**: "I need to change my income tax percentage"
- **Invoice issues**: "Invoice #1042 was denied, I need help understanding why"
- **Profile changes**: "I switched banks and need to update my account details"
- **General support**: "How do the pension contributions work exactly?"

## Creating an errand

### 1. Navigate to Errands

Open the Worknode section of your account and navigate to the **Errands** page. Here you can see all your errands, both open and closed, in one list.

### 2. Click Create Errand

Click the **Create Errand** button to start a new support conversation.

### 3. Enter the details

- **Subject**: A clear summary of your question or issue
- **Description**: A detailed explanation of what you need help with

### 4. Submit

Click **Submit** to send your errand. It is created with an **Open** status and Worknode staff are notified.

## Writing a good errand

Be specific. The more context you provide upfront, the faster Worknode can help you.

**Good subject:** "Question about pension deduction on February 2026 salary"
**Not as helpful:** "Money question"

In the description, include:

- The specific invoice number, month, or setting you are asking about
- What you expected versus what happened
- Any relevant dates or amounts

## Errand statuses

Each errand moves through these statuses as Worknode works on your request:

| Status        | Meaning                                                     |
|---------------|-------------------------------------------------------------|
| Open          | You created the errand, waiting for Worknode to pick it up  |
| In Progress   | Worknode is actively working on your request                |
| Resolved      | Worknode has addressed your question or resolved the issue  |
| Closed        | The errand is finalised and no further action is needed     |

## Message threads

Each errand has a message thread where you and Worknode staff can exchange messages. This keeps the entire conversation about a topic in one place.

When Worknode replies, you will receive a notification. You can then open the errand and continue the conversation by adding a new message.

There is no limit to how many messages an errand can have. Keep the conversation going until your question is fully answered or the issue is resolved.

## Managing multiple errands

You can have multiple errands open at the same time. Each errand is independent and has its own subject and message thread. This is useful when you have unrelated questions -- keep them in separate errands for clarity.

Your errands list shows all errands sorted by status and date, making it easy to see which ones need your attention and which are resolved.

## Tips

- **One topic per errand.** If you have two unrelated questions, create two errands. This makes it easier for Worknode to route and respond.
- **Include reference numbers.** Mention invoice numbers, dates, or amounts to help Worknode find the relevant records quickly.
- **Check existing errands first.** If you asked a similar question before, the answer might be in a previous errand.
- **Be patient but follow up.** Most errands get a reply within a business day or two. If you have not heard back after several days, add a follow-up message.

## Frequently asked questions

**What should I use errands for?**
Any Worknode-related question or issue: salary, taxes, invoices, profile changes, or general support questions.

**How fast will Worknode respond?**
Most errands receive a reply within one to two business days.

**Can I reopen a closed errand?**
You can typically add a new message to reopen the conversation, or create a new errand that references the previous one.

**Are errands private?**
Yes. Only you and Worknode staff can see your errands. Other users on your Aourly account cannot access them.

**Is there a limit to how many errands I can create?**
No. Create as many errands as you need.
