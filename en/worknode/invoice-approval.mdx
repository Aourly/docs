When you use Worknode, your invoices go through an additional approval step before being sent to clients. This is because Worknode is the legal employer and the formal sender of the invoice. They need to verify that everything is correct before the invoice goes out.

## Standard vs. Worknode invoice flow

### Standard Aourly flow (own company)

Draft --> Sent --> Paid

When you run your own company in Aourly, you create an invoice and send it directly to your client. The money goes to your own bank account.

### Worknode flow

Draft --> Pending Approval --> Approved --> Sent --> Paid

With Worknode, there is an extra step between creating the invoice and sending it. When you click Send, the invoice enters a Pending Approval status instead of going directly to the client. Worknode staff review it before it is sent.

If the invoice is denied, it can be corrected and resubmitted:

Draft --> Pending Approval --> Denied --> (edit) --> Pending Approval --> Approved --> Sent --> Paid

## Why approval is needed

Worknode is your formal employer under Swedish law. When an invoice is sent, it goes out from Worknode as the legal entity. This means:

- Worknode's organisation number and VAT number appear on the invoice
- Worknode's bank details are listed for payment (since they receive the money first via their klientmedelskonto)
- Worknode is responsible for the invoice meeting Swedish invoicing regulations

The approval step ensures that invoices are correct, compliant, and represent legitimate work before they are sent to clients on Worknode's behalf.

## Step-by-step process

### 1. Create your invoice

Create an invoice in Aourly just as you would with a regular company:

- Select the client
- Add line items (hours worked, fixed fees, expenses)
- Set the invoice date and due date
- Review the totals and VAT

The invoice starts in **Draft** status. You can edit it freely at this stage.

### 2. Submit for approval

When the invoice is ready, click **Send**. Instead of being sent directly to the client, the invoice moves to **Pending Approval** status and enters Worknode's review queue.

Before submitting, double-check:

- Client name and details are correct
- Line items accurately describe the work performed
- Amounts are reasonable and match your gig rates
- VAT is handled correctly (typically 25% for most services in Sweden)

> **Tip:** Double-check all invoice details before submitting for approval to avoid delays. Correct invoices are approved faster.

### 3. Worknode reviews the invoice

Worknode staff examine the invoice during review. They look at:

| Check                  | What they verify                                              |
|------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------|
| Client details         | Correct company name, organisation number, address            |
| Line items             | Clear descriptions, reasonable amounts                        |
| VAT handling           | Correct VAT rate applied                                      |
| Compliance             | Invoice meets Swedish legal requirements                      |

> **Info:** Invoices are typically reviewed within 1 business day. Complex invoices or those submitted during peak periods may take slightly longer.

### 4. Approved or denied

**If approved**: The invoice is automatically sent to your client. Worknode's payment details (bankgiro or klientmedelskonto) are included so the client knows where to pay.

**If denied**: You will see the reason for denial in Aourly. Common reasons include:

- Incorrect or missing client details
- Unclear line item descriptions
- VAT calculation errors
- Missing required information

When denied, correct the issues and resubmit the invoice. It goes back into the review queue.

### 5. Client pays

Once the invoice is sent, the client pays to Worknode's bank account. You can track the payment status in Aourly just like with any other invoice.

### 6. You receive your salary

After the client pays, Worknode processes the payment and pays your net salary after deducting fees, taxes, and contributions. See the [Salary & Payout](salary-and-payout) guide for a detailed breakdown.

## Invoice statuses for Worknode users

| Status              | Meaning                                                    |
|---------------------|------------------------------------------------------------|
| Draft               | Invoice is being created, not yet submitted                |
| Pending Approval    | Submitted to Worknode, waiting for review                  |
| Approved            | Worknode approved, invoice will be sent to the client      |
| Denied              | Worknode denied, corrections needed before resubmitting    |
| Sent                | Invoice has been sent to the client                        |
| Paid                | Client has paid the invoice                                |

> **Warning:** The invoice payment details will show Worknode's bank account, not yours. This is normal -- Worknode collects payment and then pays your salary.

## Tips for faster approval

- **Be thorough with client details.** Include the client's full company name, organisation number, and correct address.
- **Write clear line item descriptions.** "Web development, March 2026, 40 hours" is better than "Work done."
- **Verify VAT before submitting.** Most services in Sweden use 25% VAT, but some categories have reduced rates.
- **Submit early in the week.** Invoices submitted on Monday through Wednesday are typically reviewed faster than those submitted late on Friday.

## Frequently asked questions

**Why was my invoice denied?**
Common reasons include: incorrect VAT rate, missing client information, unclear line item descriptions, or amount discrepancies. The denial reason will be visible on the invoice so you can make corrections.

**Can I edit an invoice after submission?**
If denied, yes -- you can edit and resubmit. If already approved or sent, no -- you would need to create a credit note instead.

**How quickly are invoices reviewed?**
Usually within 1 business day.

**Whose bank details are on the invoice?**
Worknode's bank details (bankgiro or klientmedelskonto), since they receive the payment first. Your net salary is paid to your personal bank account after deductions.

**Can I cancel an invoice that has been submitted for approval?**
Check the invoice page for available actions. You may be able to withdraw the submission, make edits, and resubmit.
