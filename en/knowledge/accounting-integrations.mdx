As a business owner in Sweden, you are legally required to keep organized books. Accounting software makes this manageable, and connecting it to Aourly means your invoicing and bookkeeping stay in sync automatically.

## Why Use Accounting Software?

### It is a legal requirement

The Bokföringslag (Swedish Bookkeeping Act) requires every business to maintain organized, accurate financial records. This includes recording all transactions, keeping vouchers, and producing annual financial statements. Accounting software is the standard way to meet these obligations.

### It saves time

Manual bookkeeping -- entering every invoice, payment, and expense into spreadsheets -- is slow and error-prone. With software, most entries are automated or semi-automated.

### It reduces errors

Software enforces double-entry bookkeeping rules, validates account numbers, calculates VAT automatically, and flags inconsistencies. This means fewer mistakes in your tax returns and fewer surprises during audits.

## Popular Swedish Accounting Platforms

### Fortnox

Fortnox is the market leader in Sweden, used by over 400,000 companies. It offers a cloud-based platform covering invoicing, bookkeeping, payroll, and more. It is particularly popular among small businesses and freelancers due to its user-friendly interface and extensive ecosystem of integrations.

**Best for:** Small to medium businesses that want a comprehensive, well-supported Swedish platform.

### Visma

Visma is a large Scandinavian provider offering multiple products for different business sizes. Their offerings include Visma eEkonomi (for smaller businesses) and Visma Administration / Visma.net (for larger organizations). Visma Spiri is their newer cloud-based solution.

**Best for:** Businesses that may grow and want a platform that scales, or those already in the Visma ecosystem.

### Bjorn Lunden

Bjorn Lunden provides accounting software tailored to Swedish businesses with a focus on compliance and ease of use. It is popular among accountants and smaller firms.

**Best for:** Businesses that value a traditional, compliance-focused Swedish solution.

## What to Consider When Choosing

Before picking an accounting platform, think about:

### Your business type and size

A solo freelancer has different needs than a consulting firm with employees. Simpler solutions work well for small operations, while growing businesses may need payroll, project accounting, or multi-user access.

### Your accountant's preference

If you work with an accountant (redovisningskonsult), ask which platform they prefer. Many accountants have direct access features in Fortnox or Visma, making collaboration easier.

### Cost

Pricing varies significantly. Fortnox starts with affordable plans for small businesses, while some Visma products are priced for larger organizations. Factor in both the monthly fee and any per-transaction costs.

### Integration support

Since you are using Aourly, make sure the platform you choose is one Aourly integrates with. This ensures you get the full benefit of automated data flow between your invoicing and bookkeeping.

### Future needs

Think about where your business is heading. Will you need payroll? Multi-currency support? Inventory management? Choosing a platform that can grow with you avoids painful migrations later.

## Frequently Asked Questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Do I legally need accounting software?">
    The Bokföringslag requires all businesses to maintain organized bookkeeping. While you could theoretically do it manually, accounting software is the practical standard and greatly reduces the risk of errors.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can I switch accounting software later?">
    Yes, but it requires careful planning. You will need to export your data (often via SIE files) and import it into the new system. It is easiest to switch at the start of a new fiscal year.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="What happens if I disconnect the integration?">
    Data already synced to your accounting software remains there. New invoices and transactions from Aourly will no longer sync automatically, so you would need to enter them manually.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
